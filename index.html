The Formula

A = S × I × U

Sensing (S) captures raw input capacity.
Integration (I) captures how information is bound together.
Unity (U) captures how unified the experience is.

Key findings

Integration is the bottleneck, not sensing. The mantis shrimp has 16 photoreceptor types vs. our 3, yet has worse color discrimination. Box jellyfish have 24 eyes but minimal integration. High S with low I produces reflexive behavior, not awareness. This pattern holds across all 249 species.

Self-awareness is rare but not exclusive to mammals. Only 7 of 249 species cross the 50% threshold for self-aware tier: humans, chimpanzees, bonobos, orangutans, gorillas, bottlenose dolphins, and elephants. But corvids and octopuses come strikingly close.

SIU correlates with PCI data. Perturbational Complexity Index (the best empirical consciousness measure we have) correlates with SIU predictions at r = 0.977 across tested species.

Open questions

Where is the threshold? SIU produces a continuous scale. The tier boundaries (Minimal, Reactive, Subconscious, Conscious, Self-Aware) are working estimates, not proven thresholds. Whether awareness has hard transitions or is fully continuous is an open empirical question.

Can U be operationalized independently? Unity is the hardest parameter to measure from outside. Current proxies (EEG coherence, anesthesia response, binding measures) are indirect. Better U operationalization is the framework's biggest methodological gap.

Blinded validation needed. The 117 original species were scored by a single scorer. Blinded multi-scorer validation is the critical next step for credibility.

Epistemic status

This is a working framework, not settled science. Parameters are estimated from published behavioral and neuroscience data, but they involve scorer judgment. Confidence levels are noted for every species. The framework makes testable predictions. If the predictions fail, the framework is wrong. That's the point.
