## Executive Summary
The Integrated Awareness Framework (IAF)—also referred to as the **SIU Framework**—is a mathematical model that moves the study of consciousness from qualitative philosophy to quantifiable network science. 

Unlike Integrated Information Theory (IIT), which requires near-infinite computing power to calculate Φ, the IAF provides a **computationally tractable** formula that can be applied to real-world connectomes (from the 302 neurons of *C. elegans* to the complex networks of the human fetal brain).

## The Core Formula
The calculator implements the fundamental equation for Awareness ($A$):

$$A = (S_{ext} + S_{int}) \times I^2 \times L$$

- **$S_{ext}$ / $S_{int}$**: Sensing variables (External/Internal).
- **$I$**: Integration Score (a weighted metric of topology, gap junctions, and connectivity density).
- **$L$**: The logic/processing constant.

## Key Predictions & Benchmarks
This tool allows researchers to test the framework’s most provocative biological predictions:

1.  **The 30-Week Human Milestone**: The IAF predicts a non-linear "phase shift" in awareness at 30 weeks of human gestation, coinciding with the maturation of thalamocortical loops and cortical gap junctions.
2.  **The *C. elegans* Proof of Concept**: Demonstrates why a 302-neuron system achieves a baseline Awareness Score of ~0.65 due to high connectivity density (8.4%).
3.  **AI Discontinuity**: Quantifies why current Large Language Models (LLMs), despite high $S$ (sensing/data), lack $A$ (awareness) due to the absence of integrated, recurrent "Unity" hubs.

## Technical Implementation
This "logic playground" allows for the manipulation of:
- **Connectivity Density**: Adjusting the $K$ and $P$ values of a Small-World network.
- **Integration Weights**: Specifically the 30% weight assigned to electrical synapses (gap junctions).
- **Threshold Analysis**: Determining the "All-or-None" transition point where a system achieves Unity ($U$).

## Usage for Researchers
To verify the framework against empirical data (e.g., PhysioNet EEG records or FlyWire connectomes):
1.  Input the known connectivity parameters of the target organism.
2.  Adjust sensing variables based on sensory organ complexity.
3.  Compare the resulting Awareness Score ($A$) against Lempel-Ziv Complexity (LZC) benchmarks.

## Scientific Context
This work serves as a bridge between **Integrated Information Theory (IIT)** and **Global Neuronal Workspace Theory (GNWT)**, providing a metric that is both mathematically rigorous and empirically testable.

- **Computational Tractability**: Optimized for real-time estimation.
- **Falsifiability**: Makes specific, time-bound predictions for neonatal and comparative biology.
