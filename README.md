## The Formula

$$A = S \times I \times U$$

Three parameters, multiplied together. If any one drops to zero, awareness drops to zero.

- **S (Sensing)** — How much can it detect? Modality count, receptor quality, internal and external bandwidth. A mantis shrimp with 16 types of color receptors scores higher than a goldfish.
- **I (Integration)** — How well do those signals talk to each other? Cross-modal binding, learning, problem solving. This is the bottleneck. A box jellyfish has 24 eyes but four independent processing clusters that barely communicate — extraordinary sensing, terrible integration.
- **U (Unity)** — Is there a unified self? Does the organism act as one coherent entity with a self-model? Mirror test evidence, contingency testing, consistent behavioral identity.

**Human baseline:** S=0.85, I=0.95, U=0.95 → 100% of human waking awareness.

## The Core Insight

**Integration is the bottleneck of awareness, not brain size.**

A box jellyfish has 24 camera-type eyes (S=0.90) but scores 4.4% of human because its integration is 0.15. A cleaner wrasse has a tiny brain but recognizes individual fish by face, passes the mirror test, and conducts contingency experiments with reflections — 27.0% of human. A honeybee with a brain smaller than a sesame seed scores 48.0% because its integration is excellent.

Brain size does not predict awareness. Behavior does.

## Tier System

Organisms are classified into tiers based on functional capability, not anatomy:

| Tier | % of Human | What It Means |
|---|---|---|
| **Self-Aware III** | 80–100% | Recursive metacognition, language, cultural accumulation |
| **Self-Aware II** | 65–80% | Theory of mind, empathy, complex deception |
| **Self-Aware I** | 50–65% | Mirror recognition, contingency testing, self-model |
| **Conscious V** | 42–50% | Complex planning, cultural transmission |
| **Conscious IV** | 35–42% | Tool use, multi-step planning |
| **Conscious III** | 27–35% | Problem solving, individual recognition, strategy adjustment |
| **Conscious II** | 20–27% | Social learning, spatial memory, adapts to new situations |
| **Conscious I** | 14–20% | Basic learning, conditioning, preferences — not a sleepwalker |
| **Subconscious II** | 9–14% | Conditioned responses, habitual behavior |
| **Subconscious I** | 5–9% | Simple habituation, minimal learning |
| **Reactive** | 1–5% | Responds to stimuli but no learning |
| **Minimal** | <1% | No nervous system or equivalent |

The **sleepwalker test** determines the Conscious boundary: Can the organism learn? Adjust strategy? Pre-react to anticipated events? If yes, it's Conscious, regardless of brain size or body plan.

## Testable Predictions

Three organisms were scored *before* self-awareness testing:

- **Archer fish** — Face recognition without neocortex. Not yet mirror tested.
- **Grouper** — Referential gestures to hunting partners. Not yet mirror tested.
- **Cuttlefish** — Episodic memory and self-control. Not yet mirror tested.

If future studies confirm or deny mirror recognition in these species, those are real predictions the framework will be judged by.

## Bias Correction

Human scorers systematically overestimate mammals (especially pets) and underestimate fish, insects, and cephalopods. A bias-corrected formula (F5) applies species-specific correction factors. Parameter-level rescoring has been applied to correct for systematic underscoring of Integration and Unity in non-mammalian species — an organism that cooperates, learns, or solves problems should not score lower than a sleepwalking human regardless of its body plan.

## Open Questions

- How should S, I, U be measured objectively rather than estimated by scorers?
- Individual vs. colony scoring (ants, termites, naked mole-rats)
- Instinct vs. cognition — where is the line?
- Phase polyphenism in locusts — two different brains, two scores?

## Philosophy

This framework does not claim to solve consciousness. It claims to provide a useful, testable, quantitative estimate of awareness that produces rankings consistent with observed behavior. The tier boundaries are working estimates. The predictions are timestamped. The framework is open.

Use it, test it, break it, improve it.
